Post-Covid Travel App

Problem Statement:

Our client is interested in developing a travel app that will launch after the pandemic. We sought to understand how users can maximize their credit card travel benefits and found that Credit Card holders need a better way to book travel.

Phase 1: Research

Proto Persona

We chose to have a certain demographic based on research on individuals who are widely known for traveling and using credit cards. Mark, a financial strategist was a good fit.

View More
User Interviews

We sought to understand how Credit Card holders might maximize travel benefits post-pandemic. We conducted five interviews during this process and organized them into four topics.

View More
User Surveys

Our surveys showed that some cardholders use their rewards points to determine where to go on a trip. The data also shows that more use their card for travel transportation.

View More
Competitive Analysis

We researched similar travel apps to help us gather insights about their strengths and weaknesses. The direct competitors were Expedia and Tripit, while the indirect were AirBnB and Discover.

View More

Phase 2: Definition

Affinity Diagram

We analyzed our research and found that most people using credit cards were satisfied with gaining benefits such as cash back, reward points, or travel expenses.

View More
Empathy Map

We made an empathy map to gain a deeper insight into our potential clients needs and gains. We organized our user research in order to address all potential concerns.

View More
User Persona

We made a new persona to reflect an user-centered design approach and a potential user. We wanted to highlight the pains and gains from our empathy map.

View More
User Insight

After creating our user persona, we were able to complete our user insight on Laura. This allowed us to set up a scenario for how a user would have the need to use our app.

View More

Phase 3: Ideation

User Scenario

We wanted to choose a scenario that was related to our user research. Here we have Laura who is interviewing for a job in Finance at Sea World. She wants to spend a weekend exploring Florida.

View More
Storyboard

We were interested in solving our users problem. Laura is unexpectedly offered a job interview in Orlando and in order to pay for her travel, she signs up for a credit card.

View More
Journey Map

We wanted to outline Laura's experience and emotions while on her journey to using our travel app for credit card holders. She wants to sign up for a credit card to afford the trip.

View More
User Flow

For the sign up, the user would navigate through a splash screen, and three coaching screens. They would then choose one option out of three to sign up and then create a password.

View More

Phase 4: Prototyping

Sketches

We choose to divide up the number of sketches, I was responsible for the onboarding screens of the app. This included the splash screen, coaching screens, and sign up screens.

View More
Low-Fi Wireframes

We were able to bring our paper sketches to digital low-fidelity wireframes. We were then able to implement our user flow to show all steps included in the screens, including the login and signup.

View More
High-Fi Wireframes

We were able to transition our low-fidelity wireframes into high-fidelity. We were then able to implement our UX/UI design skills to show how this app would look in real life and the different interactions.

View More
Clickable Prototype

We were able to transition our high-fidelity wireframes into a clickable prototype. This allowed us to get ready for usability testing by creating a plan to test on five people.

Phase 5: Testing

Usability Test Plan

The goal was to the identify any usability errors concerning the onboarding process of this travel app. The hope is to learn what it takes for a user to sign up and connect their credit card.

View More
Guerilla Testing

Some issues uncovered in the usability testing of the prototype were to increase user confidence & trust by bringing the “add another credit card function” into the app.

View More
Iterate Feedback

We decided to iterate the following feedback to rename “Trip Ideas” tab to "Travel Idea" match more intuitively with users’ conception of what may be found there. This allowed for a better transition.

View More
Clickable Prototype

We were able to iterate our feedback from our guerilla testing into our clickable prototype. This allowed us to be able to complete the final version of the app to become a case study.
